The Vitaphone Soundtrack Project

The Vitaphone Soundtrack Project seeks to preserve, digitize, restore, and make freely available on the internet the surviving soundtracks from the earliest years of talking pictures (1926-1931).

Our mission is especially focused on preserving the soundtracks from early sound feature films that are otherwise lost. By saving surviving Vitaphone discs and soundtrack recordings, we hope to reunite these historic recordings with surviving film elements whenever possible.

Restoration Projects

The Vitaphone Soundtrack Project has restored and reconstructed numerous early sound films using surviving film elements, Vitaphone discs, cue sheets, research materials, and contributions from collectors and preservationists.

Vitaphone Disc Collection

We maintain a collection of digitized and preserved Vitaphone soundtrack discs from the early sound era. These recordings are being transferred, restored, and shared for research, education, and public enjoyment.

Our collections include soundtrack material from early sound films, including recordings that help reconstruct films whose original sound versions are otherwise unavailable.

Help Save Lost Soundtracks

Do you own Vitaphone discs, early soundtrack recordings, or materials from feature films released between 1926 and 1931?

We are interested in acquiring or preserving:

• Original Vitaphone discs
• Early soundtrack recordings
• Lost film soundtrack material
• Film stills and production materials
• Press books, scripts, cue sheets, and research documents

If you are unsure how to ship Vitaphone discs safely, we can provide proper packaging materials and instructions. These historic shellac records require special care to prevent damage during shipping.

Contributors may receive restoration credit and a digital copy of the preserved soundtrack or restored film when possible.

Research & Resources

Our research collection includes historical materials relating to early sound films, including:

• Sheet music
• Novelizations
• Press books
• Scripts
• Synopses
• Production information
• Historical documentation

These materials help researchers and preservationists better understand the films, performers, studios, and technology of the early sound era.
